As indicated by the large letters above this paragraph, the purpose of our project was to statistically analyze the navel flora of a random subset of the human population. The navel is essentially a scar, formed at birth by the removal of the umbilical cord. While most people (including myself) would probably not be ashamed to say they don't often think of washing their belly button out, navel hygeine goes a long way in preventing undesireable odors, appearances, and potential infection. Here's a visualization of the bacterial makeup of a few hundred persons' navels. How do you compare?
